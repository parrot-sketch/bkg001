# Surgical Case Plan - Sample Data

This document provides realistic sample data for filling in surgical case plan forms. Use these examples as templates for different types of procedures.

## Sample 1: Breast Augmentation

### Procedure Plan
Surgical Technique: Bilateral Breast Augmentation

Approach: Inframammary fold incision
Implant Placement: Submuscular (dual plane)

Step-by-Step Plan:
1. Marking: Inframammary fold at 6 o'clock position, 4cm incision length
2. Incision: Sharp dissection through skin and subcutaneous tissue
3. Pocket Creation: Submuscular dissection using electrocautery
4. Hemostasis: Meticulous hemostasis with bipolar cautery
5. Implant Insertion: 350cc silicone implants, moderate profile
6. Closure: Layered closure with absorbable sutures
7. Dressing: Sterile dressings and compression bra

### Risk Factors
Patient Risk Assessment:
- BMI: 22.5 (Normal range)
- No known allergies
- No previous breast surgery
- Non-smoker
- No significant comorbidities

Anesthetic Risk: ASA I (Healthy patient)

### Pre-op Notes
Chief Complaint:
Patient desires breast augmentation for improved breast volume and symmetry.

Examination:
Bilateral mammary hypoplasia. Good skin quality and elasticity. Symmetrical chest wall. No ptosis. Nipple-areola complex in good position.

Assessment:
Good candidate for breast augmentation. Patient has realistic expectations and understands the procedure, risks, and recovery.

Pre-operative Instructions:
- Fasting from midnight (NPO after 12:00 AM)
- No aspirin or NSAIDs 7 days prior
- Shower with antibacterial soap night before and morning of surgery
- Wear loose-fitting clothing
- Arrive at clinic by 7:00 AM

### Implant Details
- **Device Name:** Mentor MemoryGel Silicone Implant
- **Manufacturer:** Mentor Worldwide LLC
- **Lot Number:** MG-2025-001234
- **Serial Number:** SN-350-MP-001
- **Size:** 350cc Moderate Plus Profile
- **Expiry Date:** 2027-12-31

### Anesthesia Plan
**GENERAL**

### Special Instructions
Positioning:
Supine position with arms abducted at 90 degrees on arm boards.

Special Considerations:
- Patient to avoid lifting arms above shoulder level for 2 weeks
- No heavy lifting (>5kg) for 6 weeks
- Compression bra to be worn continuously for 4 weeks
- Follow-up appointment scheduled for 1 week post-op

### Estimated Duration
**120 minutes**

## Sample 2: Liposuction 360 + BBL

### Procedure Plan
Surgical Technique: Liposuction 360 + Brazilian Butt Lift

Phase 1: Liposuction 360
Harvest fat from:
- Abdomen (upper and lower)
- Flanks (love handles)
- Back (bra line area)
- Inner and outer thighs

Technique: Tumescent liposuction using power-assisted liposuction (PAL) device
Cannula Size: 3-4mm for fat harvest, 2-3mm for contouring

Phase 2: Fat Processing
Harvested fat to be processed via centrifugation. Target: 1000-1200cc processed fat.

Phase 3: Brazilian Butt Lift
Fat transfer to buttocks:
- Approximately 500cc per side
- Multi-layer injection technique
- Focus on upper pole and lateral projection

### Risk Factors
Patient Risk Assessment:
- BMI: 28.5 (Overweight - acceptable for BBL)
- No known allergies
- Previous C-section scar (lower abdomen)
- Non-smoker
- Well-controlled hypertension (on medication)

Anesthetic Risk: ASA II (Mild systemic disease)

### Pre-op Notes
Chief Complaint:
Patient desires body contouring with emphasis on waist reduction and buttock enhancement.

Examination:
Localized fat deposits in abdomen, flanks, and back. Good skin elasticity. Adequate donor fat available for BBL. Buttock shape suitable for enhancement.

Assessment:
Good candidate for combined liposuction 360 and BBL. Patient has adequate donor fat and realistic expectations.

### Implant Details
**Fat Transfer Procedure** - No implants. Approximately 1000cc processed fat harvested, 500cc per side injected into buttocks.

### Anesthesia Plan
**GENERAL**

### Special Instructions
Special Considerations:
- Patient must NOT sit directly on buttocks for 2 weeks post-op
- Use BBL pillow for sitting (provided)
- Compression garments to be worn 24/7 for 6 weeks
- No sitting, lying on back, or strenuous activity for 2 weeks
- Sleep on stomach or side only

### Estimated Duration
**180 minutes**

## Sample 3: Rhinoplasty

### Procedure Plan
Surgical Technique: Open Rhinoplasty with Septoplasty

Approach: Open rhinoplasty via transcolumellar and marginal incisions

Step-by-Step Plan:
1. Incision: Inverted-V transcolumellar incision + bilateral marginal incisions
2. Elevation: Elevate skin-soft tissue envelope
3. Dorsal Hump Reduction: Osteotome for bony hump, scissors for cartilaginous hump
4. Septoplasty: Harvest deviated septum cartilage, preserve L-strut
5. Tip Refinement: Lower lateral cartilage modification, columellar strut, tip graft
6. Osteotomies: Lateral and medial osteotomies for nasal width reduction
7. Closure: Layered closure with 6-0 PDS sutures
8. Dressing: External nasal splint, internal nasal packing

### Risk Factors
Patient Risk Assessment:
- BMI: 21.8 (Normal range)
- No known allergies
- No previous nasal surgery
- Non-smoker
- Deviated septum causing breathing difficulties

Anesthetic Risk: ASA I (Healthy patient)

### Pre-op Notes
Chief Complaint:
Nasal deformity with dorsal hump and wide nasal tip. Difficulty breathing through right nostril.

Examination:
External: Dorsal hump present, wide nasal tip, slight deviation to right. Internal: Deviated septum to right, right inferior turbinate hypertrophy.

Assessment:
Good candidate for open rhinoplasty with functional septoplasty. Patient has realistic expectations.

### Implant Details
**No implants** - Uses autologous cartilage harvested from deviated septum.

### Anesthesia Plan
**GENERAL**

### Special Instructions
Special Considerations:
- Nasal packing to remain for 24-48 hours
- External splint to remain for 7-10 days
- Patient must avoid nose blowing for 2 weeks
- Sleep with head elevated for 1 week
- Avoid glasses resting on nose bridge for 6 weeks

### Estimated Duration
**150 minutes**
